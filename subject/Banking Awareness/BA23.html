Bank Performance & Evaluation

Understanding key metrics and frameworks for assessing bank health and efficiency

Bank Performance Overview

Evaluating bank performance is crucial for regulators, investors, and customers to assess financial health, stability, and efficiency. Banks are evaluated using various financial metrics, ratios, and regulatory frameworks that provide insights into their operational effectiveness and risk management capabilities.

Essential Banking Ratios

Financial ratios provide critical insights into a bank's operational efficiency, profitability, and risk management. Regulators and analysts use these metrics to evaluate bank performance.

Capital Adequacy Ratio

Measures bank's capital in relation to its risk-weighted assets. RBI mandates minimum CAR of 9% for banks under Basel III norms.

  • Tier 1 Capital: Equity + Disclosed reserves
  • Tier 2 Capital: Undisclosed reserves + Hybrid instruments
  • Minimum requirement: 11.5% (including capital conservation buffer)

Leverage Ratio

Tier 1 capital as a percentage of bank's total exposure. Serves as a backstop to risk-based capital requirements.

  • Minimum requirement: 4% under Basel III
  • Calculated as Tier 1 Capital / Total Exposure
  • Prevents excessive leverage in banking system

Liquidity Coverage Ratio (LCR)

High-quality liquid assets as a percentage of net cash outflows over 30-day stress scenario.

  • Minimum requirement: 100%
  • Ensures short-term resilience to liquidity shocks
  • Implemented in India from 2015

Net Stable Funding Ratio (NSFR)

Available stable funding as a percentage of required stable funding over one-year horizon.

  • Minimum requirement: 100%
  • Promotes medium-term funding resilience
  • Implemented in India from 2020

Bank Evaluation Frameworks

Various regulatory frameworks and assessment models used to evaluate bank performance and financial health:

CAMELS Rating

Comprehensive supervisory rating system evaluating six components:

  • Capital Adequacy
  • Asset Quality
  • Management Capability
  • Earnings Quality
  • Liquidity Position
  • Sensitivity to Market Risk

Basel III Framework

International regulatory framework for banks focusing on:

  • Minimum capital requirements
  • Leverage ratio framework
  • Liquidity standards (LCR & NSFR)
  • Countercyclical capital buffer
  • Capital conservation buffer

RBI's Supervisory Program

RBI's comprehensive assessment approach including:

  • Annual Financial Inspection (AFI)
  • Risk-Based Supervision (RBS)
  • Prompt Corrective Action (PCA) framework
  • Early Warning Signals (EWS) monitoring
  • Thematic studies and stress testing

Deposit Insurance

DICGC protection and assessment criteria:

  • ₹5 lakh coverage per depositor per bank
  • Premium based on bank's risk profile
  • Assessment of bank's financial health
  • Early intervention mechanisms

Case Study: Public Sector Bank Turnaround

2018-2023

Analysis of a major public sector bank's performance improvement strategy following RBI's Prompt Corrective Action framework intervention.

Challenges Faced

  • High NPA levels exceeding 15% of advances
  • Negative return on assets for consecutive years
  • Inadequate capital adequacy ratio
  • Low provision coverage ratio
  • Restrictions on lending activities under PCA

Turnaround Strategies & Outcomes

  • Capital infusion through government support
  • Aggressive NPA resolution through IBC
  • Focus on retail and digital banking
  • Cost optimization and branch rationalization
  • Improved recovery rate from written-off accounts
  • Gross NPA reduced to 5.2% in 2023
  • Return to profitability with 0.7% ROA
