Consumer Grievance Redressal - Banking Ombudsman

Understanding the mechanism for resolving banking complaints in India

Banking Ombudsman Scheme Overview

The Banking Ombudsman Scheme is an expeditious and inexpensive forum for resolution of complaints against deficiency in banking services. The scheme was introduced under the Banking Regulation Act, 1949 by RBI with effect from 1995.

The current Banking Ombudsman Scheme was introduced in 2006 and updated in 2017 to include a wider range of complaints and increase the compensation limit.

Key Features of the Scheme

No Cost to Customer

The Banking Ombudsman scheme is free of charge for customers. There are no fees for filing or resolving complaints.

Legal Standing

The award passed by the Banking Ombudsman is binding on the banks but not on the complainant.

Time-Bound Resolution

The scheme mandates resolution of complaints within specific time frames, typically 30 days from receipt of complaint.

Compensation Limit

The Banking Ombudsman can award compensation up to ₹20 lakh for loss of time, expenses incurred, and mental agony.

Important Notes

  • Complaints must be filed within one year of the cause of action
  • The Banking Ombudsman may reject a complaint if it's frivolous or without sufficient cause
  • Appeal against the Ombudsman's decision can be made to the Deputy Governor of RBI within 30 days

Types of Grievances Covered

The Banking Ombudsman can consider complaints relating to the following deficiency in banking services:

Loan & Advances

  • Non-observance of RBI directives on interest rates
  • Non-sanction of loans without valid reasons
  • Delay in sanction of loans
  • Non-acceptance of loan application without valid reason

Deposit Accounts

  • Non-payment or delay in payment of cheques
  • Failure to honor guarantee or letter of credit
  • Premature closure of fixed deposits without consent
  • Non-adherence to instructions in mandate or power of attorney

Charges & Fees

  • Levying of charges without prior notice
  • Non-adherence to prescribed schedule of charges
  • Non-transparency in charges
  • Unauthorized deductions from accounts

Digital Banking

  • Failed ATM transactions
  • Issues with internet banking
  • Mobile banking complaints
  • Debit/credit card related issues

Pension Payments

  • Delay or failure to make pension payments
  • Refusal to accept application for pension
  • Non-observance of government guidelines

Remittances

  • Delay in remittances abroad
  • Failure to issue drafts/pay orders
  • Non-adherence to prescribed time norms
  • Charges for non-home branch transactions

Jurisdiction of Banking Ombudsman

The Banking Ombudsman's jurisdiction is determined by the location of the branch where the transaction occurred or the residence of the complainant.

Criteria Jurisdiction Notes
Branch Location Ombudsman of the state where the branch is located For branch-specific complaints
Complainant's Residence Ombudsman of the state where complainant resides If transaction happened online or through other channels
Multiple Locations Ombudsman of any related state For complaints involving multiple branches
Central Government Employees Ombudsman of state where pension is disbursed Special provision for pension-related complaints

Type of Compensation Maximum Limit Applicable For
Monetary Loss Actual amount + interest Direct financial losses
Mental Agony & Harassment ₹1 lakh Deficiency in service causing distress
Total Compensation ₹20 lakh Overall limit per complaint
Costs incurred Actual expenses Documented expenses related to complaint

Case Study Example

Unauthorized Credit Card Charges

Mr. Sharma noticed unauthorized transactions totaling ₹85,000 on his credit card statement. He immediately reported it to his bank but the bank failed to resolve the issue for 45 days.

Complaint Filed

Mr. Sharma approached the Banking Ombudsman after the bank's unsatisfactory response.

Ombudsman's Findings

The bank had not followed RBI guidelines on reporting and resolving fraudulent transactions within the stipulated time frame.

Resolution

The Ombudsman directed the bank to reverse the unauthorized charges, waive the interest, and pay ₹10,000 as compensation for mental harassment.

Time Taken

The entire process was completed within 30 days of filing the complaint with the Ombudsman.
