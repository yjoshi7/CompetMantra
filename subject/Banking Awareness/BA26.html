Banking Awareness

Narasimham Committee II (1998)

Detailed analysis of recommendations 9-11 for banking sector reforms

Committee Background

The Narasimham Committee II was formed in 1998 under the chairmanship of M. Narasimham, former RBI Governor, to review the progress of banking sector reforms and suggest further measures to strengthen the Indian financial system. The committee submitted its report in April 1998 with 11 key recommendations.

Committee Details

  • Formed: 1998
  • Chairman: M. Narasimham (Former RBI Governor)
  • Focus: Further strengthening of financial institutions
  • Key Areas: Capital adequacy, asset quality, governance, systemic stability
  • Total Recommendations: 11

Implementation Timeline

Key milestones in the implementation of Narasimham Committee II recommendations:

1998
Committee Report Submission

Narasimham Committee II submits its report with 11 recommendations for banking sector reforms

1999
Asset Reconstruction Company Proposal

Initial discussions on setting up ARCs based on Recommendation 9

2002
SARFAESI Act Enactment

Parliament passes the Securitisation and Reconstruction of Financial Assets and Enforcement of Security Interest Act

2003
First ARC Established

Asset Reconstruction Company (India) Limited (ARCIL) becomes the first ARC to commence operations

2004
New Bankruptcy Framework Discussions

Initial discussions on comprehensive bankruptcy law as suggested in Recommendation 11

2016
Insolvency and Bankruptcy Code

Parliament passes the Insolvency and Bankruptcy Code, implementing the spirit of Recommendation 11

9

Asset Reconstruction Companies (ARCs)

The committee recommended the establishment of Asset Reconstruction Companies (ARCs) to take over non-performing assets (NPAs) from banks and financial institutions. These specialized entities would focus on recovery and resolution of stressed assets.

Key Objectives

  • Clean up bank balance sheets by transferring NPAs
  • Specialize in recovery mechanisms
  • Enable banks to focus on core banking activities
  • Improve overall financial system efficiency

Implementation Mechanism

  • Legal framework for ARCs through SARFAESI Act, 2002
  • Power to acquire financial assets from banks
  • Authority to issue Security Receipts to qualified buyers
  • Enforcement powers without court intervention

Outcomes

  • Establishment of multiple ARCs in India
  • Recovery of significant amounts from NPAs
  • Improved NPA resolution mechanisms
  • Development of secondary market for distressed assets

Impact on Banking Sector

The creation of ARCs fundamentally changed how banks manage non-performing assets. It provided a specialized mechanism for recovery, reduced the burden on banks' balance sheets, and introduced professional asset reconstruction practices in India.

Implementation Status: Fully implemented through SARFAESI Act, 2002
10

Bank Ownership and Capital Adequacy

The committee recommended reducing government stake in public sector banks to 33% and increasing capital adequacy standards. This was aimed at improving governance, bringing in market discipline, and strengthening banks' capital base.

Capital Adequacy Enhancements

  • Increase capital to risk-weighted assets ratio (CRAR)
  • Gradual increase to international standards
  • Proper provisioning for NPAs and standard assets
  • Introduction of tier-based capital structure

Ownership Reforms

  • Reduce government stake to 33% in PSBs
  • Greater autonomy in banking operations
  • Professionalization of bank boards
  • Improved corporate governance standards

Implementation Progress

  • Partial reduction in government stake in some PSBs
  • CRAR standards aligned with Basel norms
  • Introduction of differentiated banking licenses
  • Improved disclosure and transparency norms

Impact on Banking Sector

While the government stake reduction has been gradual, capital adequacy norms have been significantly strengthened. Indian banks now maintain higher capital buffers, and governance standards have improved, though complete disinvestment remains a work in progress.

Implementation Status: Partially implemented - capital norms strengthened but government stake reduction incomplete
11

Legal Reforms and Bankruptcy Framework

The committee emphasized the need for a strong legal framework for loan recovery and bankruptcy proceedings. It recommended changes to existing laws and creation of new mechanisms for faster resolution of stressed assets.

Legal Framework Changes

  • Reform of Debt Recovery Tribunals (DRTs)
  • Strengthening of SARFAESI Act provisions
  • Introduction of wilful default concept
  • Faster enforcement of security interests

Bankruptcy Code Development

  • Comprehensive bankruptcy legislation
  • Time-bound resolution process
  • Insolvency professionals ecosystem
  • Information utilities for financial data

Outcomes Achieved

  • Establishment of Insolvency and Bankruptcy Code, 2016
  • National Company Law Tribunal (NCLT) for resolution
  • Improved recovery rates through IBC
  • Cultural shift in credit discipline

Impact on Banking Sector

The implementation of a comprehensive bankruptcy framework through IBC has been transformative for the Indian financial system. It has significantly improved recovery rates, reduced resolution time, and changed borrower behavior through the threat of loss of control.

Implementation Status: Fully implemented through Insolvency and Bankruptcy Code, 2016

Comparative Analysis of Recommendations 9-11

Implementation status and impact assessment of the three key recommendations:

Recommendation Primary Focus Implementation Mechanism Status Impact Rating
#9: Asset Reconstruction Companies NPA Resolution SARFAESI Act, 2002 Fully Implemented High
#10: Bank Ownership & Capital Governance & Stability Gradual reforms Partially Implemented Medium
#11: Legal Reforms Bankruptcy Framework IBC, 2016 Fully Implemented Very High

Overall Impact of Recommendations 9-11

The implementation of these three recommendations has significantly transformed India's banking sector:

Financial Stability

  • Stronger capital buffers in banks
  • Improved ability to absorb shocks
  • Reduced systemic risk from NPAs
  • Better risk management practices

Recovery Mechanisms

  • Specialized ARCs for NPA resolution
  • Faster recovery through SARFAESI
  • Time-bound resolution under IBC
  • Improved recovery rates

Governance & Discipline

  • Market discipline through reduced government stake
  • Professional board composition
  • Improved credit culture
  • Reduced moral hazard

Key Takeaways

  • Narasimham Committee II laid the foundation for modern banking reforms
  • Recommendations 9-11 addressed critical gaps in the financial system
  • The IBC has been one of the most significant financial reforms in India
  • Implementation of these recommendations strengthened India's banking sector significantly
