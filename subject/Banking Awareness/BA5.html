Types of Accounts & Deposits

Understanding different banking accounts and deposit options available in India

Banking Accounts & Deposits Overview

Bank accounts and deposits form the foundation of banking services, allowing individuals and businesses to safely store money, earn interest, and access various financial services. In banking terms, deposits represent liabilities for banks (as they owe this money to customers) while loans represent assets (as they generate income for the bank).

The Banking Flow: Deposits to Loans

Visual representation of how customer deposits become the source for bank loans:


Customer Deposits

Bank's Liability (They owe this money back to customers)


Bank Operations

Maintains reserves (CRR/SLR) and lends the rest


Loans to Customers

Bank's Asset (Generates income through interest)

Key Points About This Flow

  • Deposits are safe: Insured up to ₹5 lakhs by DICGC
  • Interest earned: Deposits earn interest while loans cost interest
  • Fractional reserve banking: Banks keep only a fraction of deposits as reserves
  • Liquidity transformation: Short-term deposits fund long-term loans

Types of Bank Accounts

Banks offer various account types to cater to different customer needs, from daily transactions to long-term savings.

1. Demand Deposit Accounts

Accounts where money can be withdrawn at any time without prior notice to the bank.

Savings Account

  • Designed for personal savings
  • Interest rates: 2.5-7% p.a.
  • Minimum balance requirements vary
  • Limited number of free transactions
  • ATM/debit card provided

Current Account

  • For businesses and frequent transactions
  • No interest earned on balance
  • No limit on transactions
  • Higher minimum balance requirements
  • Overdraft facility available

Savings vs Current Account

Feature Savings Account Current Account
Purpose Personal savings Business transactions
Interest 2.5-7% p.a. Usually none
Transactions Limited free transactions Unlimited transactions
Minimum Balance ₹500-₹10,000 ₹5,000-₹1,00,000+
Overdraft Not available Available

2. Term/Time Period Accounts

Accounts where money is deposited for a fixed period at higher interest rates than savings accounts.

Fixed Deposit (FD)

  • Fixed amount for fixed tenure (7 days-10 years)
  • Higher interest rates (4-8% p.a.)
  • Premature withdrawal penalties
  • Taxable interest (TDS if interest > ₹40,000/₹50,000)
  • Loan against FD available

Recurring Deposit (RD)

  • Fixed monthly deposits for fixed tenure
  • Interest rates similar to FDs
  • Tenure usually 6 months-10 years
  • Premature closure penalties
  • Good for disciplined savings

FD vs RD Accounts

Feature Fixed Deposit Recurring Deposit
Deposit Type Lump sum Monthly installments
Interest Calculation On entire amount On monthly balances
Minimum Amount ₹1,000-₹10,000 ₹100-₹1,000/month
Flexibility Fixed amount Fixed monthly payments
Best For Lump sum investment Regular savings habit

Types of Deposits in Indian Banks

Beyond basic accounts, banks offer specialized deposit products catering to specific customer needs.

Salary Account

  • Opened by employers for employees
  • Zero balance maintenance
  • Salary credited directly
  • Special benefits like free drafts, higher ATM limits
  • Converts to normal savings if salary stops

Demat Account

  • For holding shares & securities electronically
  • Mandatory for stock market trading
  • Linked with trading account
  • Annual maintenance charges apply
  • DP (Depository Participant) maintains it

Dormant Account

  • Inactive for 2+ years
  • No customer-initiated transactions
  • Bank may charge penalties
  • Can be reactivated with KYC
  • After 10 years, transferred to DEAF fund

NRI Accounts

NRE Account

  • Foreign income deposited in INR
  • Tax-free in India
  • Repatriable (can send back abroad)

NRO Account

  • For Indian income of NRIs
  • Taxable in India
  • Limited repatriation

FCNR Account

  • Fixed deposits in foreign currency
  • No exchange rate risk
  • Repatriable

Benefits of Different Account Types

Choosing the right account type can help maximize benefits based on your financial needs and goals.

Safety & Security

Bank accounts are much safer than keeping cash at home, with deposit insurance up to ₹5 lakhs per customer per bank.

Interest Earnings

Savings and deposit accounts help your money grow through interest, combating inflation.

Convenience

24/7 access through ATMs, internet banking, and mobile apps for seamless transactions.

Financial Discipline

Recurring deposits and systematic plans help build regular savings habits.

Credit Opportunities

Maintaining accounts improves banking relationship, helping secure loans when needed.

Transaction Records

Bank statements serve as financial records for tax purposes and loan applications.

Choosing the Right Account: Practical Tips

For Salaried Individuals

  • Salary account + savings account combo
  • FD/RD for short-term goals
  • Demat account if investing in stocks
  • Automated transfers to savings

For Business Owners

  • Current account for transactions
  • Separate savings for personal use
  • FDs for tax planning
  • Higher limit accounts for large transactions

For Students/Seniors

  • Zero-balance student accounts
  • Senior citizen accounts with higher interest
  • Joint accounts for easy access
  • Nomination facility important

For NRIs

  • NRE for foreign income (tax-free)
  • NRO for Indian income
  • FCNR for foreign currency deposits
  • Consider repatriation needs

Final Lesson: Account Selection Strategy

  • Assess your needs: Daily banking, savings, or investments?
  • Compare features: Interest rates, fees, minimum balance
  • Consider convenience: Branch network, digital features
  • Plan for the future: Scalability for growing needs
