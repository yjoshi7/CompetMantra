Cards & Banking Instruments

Understanding payment cards, cheques, and demand drafts in modern banking

Payment Instruments Overview

Modern banking offers various instruments for transactions, savings, and payments. These instruments facilitate the flow of money in the economy and provide convenience, security, and flexibility to customers. The three primary categories are payment cards (debit/credit), cheques, and demand drafts.

Banking Instruments

Explore the different banking instruments that enable financial transactions:

Debit & Credit Cards

Plastic cards that enable electronic access to financial resources. Debit cards access your own funds, while credit cards provide borrowed funds up to a limit.

Key Features

  • Electronic payment convenience
  • Global acceptance at merchants
  • ATM cash withdrawal capability
  • Security features (PIN, OTP, chip)
  • Rewards and cashback programs
  • Online transaction capability

Cheques

A written document that instructs a bank to pay a specific amount of money from a person's account to the person in whose name the cheque has been issued.

Key Features

  • Written order to bank for payment
  • Valid for 3 months from issue date
  • Various types: bearer, order, crossed
  • Requires signature matching records
  • MICR technology for processing
  • Can be canceled via stop payment

Demand Drafts

A negotiable instrument issued by a bank directing another bank branch to pay a specified amount to the designated payee on demand.

Key Features

  • Prepaid instrument with guaranteed payment
  • Safer than carrying cash for large amounts
  • Not payable to bearer (always to order)
  • Valid for 3 months from issue date
  • Can be canceled with fee payment
  • Requires bank account to purchase

Security Measures

Protecting your banking instruments is crucial to prevent financial fraud and loss:

Card Security

  • Never share your PIN with anyone
  • Enable transaction alerts on your phone
  • Use virtual cards for online transactions
  • Immediately report lost/stolen cards
  • Check statements regularly for unauthorized transactions

Cheque Security

  • Keep cheque books in a secure place
  • Use "A/C Payee" crossing to prevent misuse
  • Avoid signing blank cheques
  • Reconcile cheques issued with bank statements
  • Use dark ink that cannot be easily altered

Draft Security

  • Keep demand drafts like cash until delivered
  • Send through secure channels when mailing
  • Note down draft details for tracking
  • Ensure payee name is correctly spelled
  • Cancel lost drafts immediately with the bank

Cheque Clearing Process

Understanding how cheques move through the banking system:

1

Issuance

Drawer writes and signs cheque to payee

2

Deposit

Payee deposits cheque in their bank

3

Processing

Cheque sent to clearing house via presenting bank

4

Verification

Drawee bank verifies signature and funds

5

Settlement

Funds transferred between banks

Important Note

The cheque clearing cycle typically takes 2-3 business days. With the introduction of Cheque Truncation System (CTS), physical movement of cheques has been replaced by digital image transmission, speeding up the process significantly.
