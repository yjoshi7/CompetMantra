Banking Awareness

Deposit Insurance and Credit Guarantee Corporation (DICGC)

Protecting depositors and strengthening the banking system

Introduction to DICGC

The Deposit Insurance and Credit Guarantee Corporation (DICGC) is a wholly-owned subsidiary of the Reserve Bank of India (RBI) established under the DICGC Act, 1961. It provides insurance coverage for bank deposits and guarantees credit facilities to protect depositors and strengthen the banking system.

1961

DICGC was established through the enactment of the Deposit Insurance Corporation Act, 1961.

1978

The Corporation was renamed to Deposit Insurance and Credit Guarantee Corporation (DICGC) after merging the Credit Guarantee Corporation of India with it.

Functions and Objectives

Deposit Insurance

Providing insurance coverage for bank deposits to protect small depositors in the event of bank failure.

Credit Guarantee

Offering guarantees for credit facilities extended to certain categories of small borrowers, primarily to encourage lending to priority sectors.

Risk Monitoring

Monitoring the risk profile of insured banks and taking appropriate measures to mitigate potential losses to the Deposit Insurance Fund.

Awareness Creation

Creating awareness among depositors about the deposit insurance system and its benefits.

Insurance Coverage Details

DICGC provides insurance coverage for deposits held in different types of banks. The coverage is automatic and doesn't require any application from depositors.

Coverage Limit

Each depositor is insured up to ₹5,00,000 (₹5 lakh) for both principal and interest amounts across all accounts in the same bank.

Covered Banks

All commercial banks, regional rural banks, local area banks, and cooperative banks are insured by DICGC.

Types of Deposits

Coverage includes savings, fixed, current, recurring deposits, and accrued interest.

Non-Covered Items

Deposits of foreign governments, deposits of central/state governments, and inter-bank deposits are not insured.

Premium Structure

Banks pay insurance premium to DICGC based on their assessable deposits. The premium rates are determined by the Corporation with approval from RBI.

Period Premium Rate (per ₹100 of assessable deposits) Remarks
1962-1968 0.05 paise (₹0.0005) Initial rate
1968-1971 0.06 paise (₹0.0006) First increase
1971-1993 0.08 paise (₹0.0008) Second increase
1993-2005 0.10 paise (₹0.0010) Third increase
2005-2020 0.10 paise (₹0.0010) No change
2020-Present 0.12 paise (₹0.0012) Increased after 15 years

Claim Process

When a bank fails, DICGC initiates the claim settlement process to compensate depositors up to the insured amount.

Step 1: Bank Failure Declaration

RBI declares that the bank has failed and will not be able to repay its depositors.

Step 2: Data Collection

DICGC collects information about depositors and their deposits from the failed bank.

Step 3: Claim Calculation

DICGC calculates the insurance amount payable to each depositor (up to ₹5 lakh).

Step 4: Payment

Insurance amount is paid either directly to depositors or through another bank appointed by RBI.

Step 5: Recovery

DICGC attempts to recover the amount paid from the assets of the failed bank.

Recent Developments

Increased Coverage

In 2020, the insurance coverage was increased from ₹1,00,000 to ₹5,00,000 per depositor per bank.

Awareness Campaigns

DICGC has intensified its efforts to create awareness about deposit insurance among the public.

Premium Rate Adjustment

The premium rate was increased from 10 paise to 12 paise per ₹100 of assessable deposits in 2020.

Digital Initiatives

DICGC is working on digital systems for faster claim settlement and better monitoring of insured banks.

Key Takeaways

  • DICGC provides insurance coverage of up to ₹5 lakh per depositor per bank
  • Coverage is automatic and doesn't require any application from depositors
  • All commercial banks, RRBs, and cooperative banks are insured by DICGC
  • The premium is paid by banks, not by depositors
  • DICGC plays a crucial role in maintaining confidence in the banking system
Did You Know?

Before the increase in 2020, the deposit insurance cover had remained at ₹1 lakh for nearly 27 years since 1993.
