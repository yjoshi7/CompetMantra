Money Market in India

Understanding the short-term debt instruments and their role in the financial system

Money Market Overview

The money market is a segment of the financial market where financial instruments with high liquidity and very short maturities are traded. It is used by participants as a means for borrowing and lending in the short term, from several days to just under a year.

Key Characteristics of Money Market

Short-Term Nature

Instruments have maturities ranging from overnight to one year, providing short-term funding solutions.

High Safety

Instruments are generally issued by entities with high credit ratings, ensuring low default risk.

High Liquidity

Money market instruments can be quickly converted to cash with minimal price impact.

Money Market Instruments

The Indian money market consists of various instruments that cater to different short-term funding needs of financial institutions, corporations, and the government.

Treasury Bills (T-Bills)

Short-term instruments issued by the Government of India to finance its short-term requirements.

Maturities: 91, 182, and 364 days
Issued at discount and redeemed at face value

Commercial Paper (CP)

Unsecured money market instrument issued in the form of a promissory note by corporate entities.

Maturity: 7 days to 1 year
Minimum credit rating of A2 required

Certificate of Deposit (CD)

Negotiable money market instrument issued by banks and financial institutions.

Maturity: 7 days to 1 year
Issued at discount to face value

Commercial Bill

Short-term, negotiable, and self-liquidating instrument with low risk.

Maturity: 90 days (typically)
Arises from trade transactions

Call Money

Short-term finance repayable on demand, with a maturity period of 1 to 14 days.

Overnight to 14 days maturity
Inter-bank market for short-term funds

Notice Money

Money borrowed or lent for a period between 2 days and 14 days.

Requires 2-14 days notice for withdrawal
Maturity beyond call money but short-term

Money Market Participants

The money market consists of various participants who either supply funds or borrow funds for short durations.

RBI's Role in Money Market

Regulator: Oversees and regulates money market operations and participants
Lender of Last Resort: Provides emergency funding to banks facing temporary liquidity shortages
Conducts OMOs: Uses Open Market Operations to manage liquidity in the system
Issuer of T-Bills: Issues Treasury Bills on behalf of the government

Money Market vs. Capital Market

While both money market and capital market are components of the financial market, they serve different purposes and have distinct characteristics.

Parameter Money Market Capital Market
Time Horizon Short-term (up to 1 year) Long-term (over 1 year)
Instruments T-bills, Commercial Paper, CDs, etc. Stocks, Bonds, Debentures, etc.
Liquidity High liquidity Relatively less liquid
Risk Factor Low risk Higher risk
Purpose Short-term funding needs Long-term investment
Return on Investment Lower returns Higher potential returns
Participants Banks, financial institutions, corporations Investors, companies, institutions

Functions and Importance of Money Market

The money market plays several crucial roles in the financial system and overall economy.

Liquidity Management

Helps banks and financial institutions manage their short-term liquidity needs efficiently.

Implementation of Monetary Policy

Provides the platform for central banks to implement monetary policy through various operations.

Short-Term Funding

Enables governments and corporations to meet their short-term funding requirements at competitive rates.

Economic Indicator

Money market rates serve as important indicators of overall economic health and liquidity conditions.
