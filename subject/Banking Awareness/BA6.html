Types of Accounts & Deposits

Understanding different banking products and their classifications

Banking Products Overview

Banks offer various types of accounts and deposit products to cater to different customer needs. These products can be broadly classified into secured and unsecured categories, each with specific features, benefits, and risk profiles.

Key Differences

  • Secured Loans require collateral and typically have lower interest rates
  • Unsecured Loans don't require collateral but have higher interest rates
  • Loan approval depends on credit history, income, and repayment capacity
  • Different loans serve different purposes with specific terms and conditions

Secured Loans

Loans backed by collateral or security that the borrower pledges to the lender. If the borrower defaults, the lender can seize the collateral to recover the loan amount.

Mortgage Loans

Loans where immovable property is used as collateral without physically handing it over to the bank.

Characteristics

  • Collateral remains with borrower
  • Against immovable property
  • Long repayment tenure
  • Lower interest rates

Examples

  • Housing Loans
  • Loans Against Property (LAP)
  • Commercial Real Estate Loans

Documentation

  • Property title documents
  • Valuation reports
  • Legal verification
  • Mortgage registration

Hypothecation Loans

Loans where movable assets are used as collateral while the borrower retains possession of the assets.

Characteristics

  • Collateral remains with borrower
  • Against movable property
  • Medium repayment tenure
  • Moderate interest rates

Examples

  • Vehicle Loans (Car, Bike)
  • Machinery Loans
  • Equipment Financing

Documentation

  • Asset purchase invoices
  • Insurance policies
  • Hypothecation agreement
  • RC book for vehicles

Pledge Loans

Loans where physical possession of movable collateral is transferred to the lender until the loan is repaid.

Characteristics

  • Collateral handed over to bank
  • Against movable assets
  • Short to medium tenure
  • Lowest interest rates among secured loans

Examples

  • Gold Loans
  • Jewelry Loans
  • Securities Pledge (Shares, Bonds)

Documentation

  • Asset ownership proof
  • Valuation certificate
  • Pledge agreement
  • Safe custody receipt

Unsecured Loans

Loans provided without any collateral security, based solely on the borrower's creditworthiness and repayment capacity.

Personal Loans

  • No collateral required
  • Based on credit score and income
  • Quick disbursal
  • Higher interest rates
  • Flexible end-use

Short-term Business Loans

  • Tenure up to 1 year
  • For working capital needs
  • Business vintage requirements
  • Cash flow based assessment

Education Loans

  • For educational expenses
  • Up to ₹20 lakh for abroad studies
  • Up to ₹15 lakh for India studies
  • Moratorium period available
  • Lower interest rates for meritorious students

Consumer Durable Loans

  • For white goods and electronics
  • Point-of-sale financing
  • Low documentation
  • Tenure up to 2 years

Agricultural Loans

  • For farming activities
  • Subsidized interest rates
  • Crop cycle based repayment
  • Government schemes available

Home Renovation Loans

  • For home improvement
  • Based on property value
  • Tenure up to 7 years
  • Quicker than top-up loans

Non-Performing Assets (NPA)

When a borrower fails to make principal or interest payments for 90 days, the loan is classified as a Non-Performing Asset (NPA).

Standard Asset
Regular payments - No delays in EMI payments
SMA-0
Special Mention Account - 0 - Payments overdue for 0-30 days
SMA-1
Special Mention Account - 1 - Payments overdue for 31-60 days
SMA-2
Special Mention Account - 2 - Payments overdue for 61-90 days
NPA
Non-Performing Asset - Payments overdue for more than 90 days

NPA Classification

Substandard Asset

  • NPA for less than 12 months
  • Current net worth insufficient
  • Well-defined credit weaknesses

Doubtful Asset

  • NPA for more than 12 months
  • Collection in full questionable
  • High probability of loss

Loss Asset

  • Considered uncollectible
  • Little recovery value
  • Remains on books until written off

EMI - Equated Monthly Installment

EMI is a fixed payment amount made by a borrower to a lender at a specified date each calendar month. EMIs are used to pay off both interest and principal each month.

EMI Components

  • Principal: Original loan amount
  • Interest: Cost of borrowing
  • Tenure: Loan repayment period
  • Interest Rate: Annual rate charged

EMI Calculation Formula

EMI = P × r × (1 + r)^n / ((1 + r)^n - 1)

  • P = Principal loan amount
  • r = Monthly interest rate
  • n = Loan tenure in months

Factors Affecting EMI

  • Loan amount
  • Interest rate
  • Loan tenure
  • Type of interest (fixed vs floating)
  • Processing fees and other charges

DICGC - Deposit Insurance and Credit Guarantee Corporation

DICGC protects bank depositors by insuring their deposits in the event of a bank failure.

Establishment

Established on July 15, 1978, as a subsidiary of RBI

Insurance Coverage

₹5 lakh per depositor per bank (including principal and interest)

Legal Basis

Operates under DICGC Act, 1961

Premium

12 paise per ₹100 per year (0.12% annually)

Merger History

Formed by merging Deposit Insurance Corporation (DIC) and Credit Guarantee Corporation of India (CGCI)

Covered Deposits

  • Savings accounts
  • Fixed deposits
  • Current accounts
  • Recurring deposits

Not Covered by DICGC

  • Deposits of foreign governments
  • Deposits of central/state governments
  • Inter-bank deposits
  • Deposits received outside India
  • Amounts specifically exempted by DICGC
