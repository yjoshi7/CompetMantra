National Housing Bank (NHB)

India's apex financial institution for housing finance

Overview of NHB

The National Housing Bank (NHB) is India's apex financial institution for housing, established on July 9, 1988, under the National Housing Bank Act, 1987. It operates as a wholly-owned subsidiary of the Reserve Bank of India (RBI) and serves as the principal agency to promote housing finance institutions both at local and regional levels.

Key Facts

  • Established: July 9, 1988
  • Headquarters: New Delhi
  • Parent Organization: Reserve Bank of India (RBI)
  • Chairman & Managing Director: Shri R. V. Verma
  • Primary Role: Regulation and supervision of Housing Finance Companies (HFCs)

Mission & Vision

NHB's mission is to harness and promote the housing potential in the country by developing a sound, healthy, and cost-effective housing finance system to cater to all segments of the population. Its vision is to facilitate inclusive growth with stability in the housing finance market.

Functions of NHB

The National Housing Bank performs a wide range of functions to promote a robust housing finance ecosystem in India:

Financial Function

  • Extending refinance to eligible housing finance institutions
  • Providing loans and advances to scheduled banks and housing finance institutions
  • Formulating schemes for the purpose of mobilization of resources and extension of credit for housing

Promotional Function

  • Promoting and developing specialized housing finance institutions
  • Encouraging the establishment of a network of dedicated housing finance institutions
  • Providing technical and administrative assistance to housing finance institutions

Regulatory Function

  • Regulating the housing finance system in the country
  • Supervising the operations of housing finance companies
  • Issuing directions to housing finance institutions to ensure their growth on sound lines

Rural Housing Fund (RHF)

The Rural Housing Fund was established to boost rural housing finance by providing refinance support to primary lending institutions for loans extended by them for housing purposes in rural areas.

  • Focuses on economically weaker sections and low-income groups in rural areas
  • Provides refinance at concessional rates
  • Supports construction of new houses and improvement of existing ones

Affordable Housing Fund (AHF)

The Affordable Housing Fund aims to enhance credit flow to the affordable housing segment by providing refinance support to eligible institutions.

  • Targets urban and semi-urban areas
  • Focuses on economically weaker sections and low/middle income groups
  • Supports the Pradhan Mantri Awas Yojana (PMAY) mission

Disaster Relief Fund

This fund provides assistance for reconstruction of houses damaged by natural calamities like floods, earthquakes, cyclones, etc.

  • Provides refinance support for repair/construction of damaged houses
  • Offers concessional interest rates
  • Works in coordination with state governments and disaster management authorities

NHB RESIDEX

NHB RESIDEX is India's first official housing price index that tracks movement in residential property prices across major cities.

  • Provides an indicator of market trends in housing prices
  • Covers 50 major cities across India
  • Helps in policy formulation and investment decisions
  • Updated quarterly

Organizational Structure

The National Housing Bank operates under the guidance of a Board of Directors with representatives from the Government of India, Reserve Bank of India, housing finance institutions, and experts in housing and urban development.
